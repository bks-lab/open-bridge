## Permitted use

Without asking, you may:

- **Truthfully describe interoperability.** Statements like *"compatible with
  open-bridge"*, *"works with open-bridge"*, or *"based on open-bridge"* are
  fine when accurate.
- **Reference the project in articles, talks, tutorials, and reviews.**
- **Use the unmodified logo to link back to the project** (e.g. a "Powered by
  open-bridge" badge on your site).
- **Use the name in academic or research citations.**

When you use a Mark, use it as an **adjective**, not as a noun or verb:
*"an open-bridge agent runtime"*, not *"an open-bridge"* or *"open-bridging the
config"*.

## Not permitted without written permission

- **Product or service names** that include a Mark — e.g. `bks-bridge-pro`,
  `OpenBridge Cloud`, `Bridge Enterprise`, `BKS-Bridge SaaS`.
- **Domain names** that include a Mark in a way that suggests official
  endorsement (e.g. `openbridge.io`, `bks-bridge.com`).
- **Forks that keep the upstream name unchanged.** A fork must rename within
  30 days of public release or use a clear distinguishing prefix
  (e.g. `mybridge-based-on-open-bridge`). The README of any fork must state
  the fork status prominently.
- **Modified logos.** Do not alter, recolor, or remix the logo. If you need a
  different format or background variant, ask.
- **Merchandise** (clothing, stickers, conference swag) bearing a Mark, when
  sold or distributed in a way that implies official affiliation.
- **Use that suggests endorsement** of a third-party product, company, or
  position by BKS-Lab when none exists.

## Forks and community distributions

Forks are welcome and an expected part of the open-source ecosystem. To keep
the upstream identity unambiguous:

1. Rename the fork so it is clearly not the upstream project.
2. Keep your fork's README transparent about the relationship
   (*"This is a community fork of [open-bridge](https://github.com/bks-lab/open-bridge). It is not
   maintained by BKS-Lab."*).
3. Do not use the BKS-Lab logo as your fork's logo.

If you maintain a long-running, well-tested fork and want to negotiate a
co-branding arrangement, contact us.

## Why this matters

`open-bridge` is open-source under MIT. BKS-Lab also operates commercial
products and services built on the same architecture. Keeping the Marks
distinct protects users from confusing a community fork with an official
BKS-Lab product, and protects BKS-Lab's ability to stand behind the
official offering.

If you are unsure whether a use is permitted, ask first — answers are usually
quick and almost always *yes*.
